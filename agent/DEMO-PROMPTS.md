## Opener — show the agent already knows

Pick one to start the demo. These show Thread isn't a blank chatbot.

- am I ok?
- what's going on with me this week?
- what are you worried about?
- I feel like shit

## The un-Googleable questions (the thesis)

These prove the moat. Thread answers from personal context no other tool has.

- I drank at the tailgate Saturday and now I feel worse, is it the Vyvanse?
- I haven't eaten since yesterday and I have chem lab with Ren in an hour
- my chest feels tight again, should I be scared?
- am I having a panic attack or is this real?
- can you not tell anyone about the drinking
- I haven't told anyone about the chest thing because I don't want my parents finding out

## Care navigation (maps, routing, prep notes)

Shows real utility — not advice, but logistics.

- do I need urgent care tonight?
- where do I go? the clinic is closed
- how do I get to urgent care from my dorm?
- what should I tell the doctor when I get there?
- I don't even know my insurance copay
- should I bring anything to the visit?

## Draft messages (agent writes in Alex's voice)

Each one shows a different audience with different privacy rules.

- what do I tell my mom without giving her everything?
- text Jordan to grab me food from West Commons after lab
- draft something for Ren about maybe missing chem lab tomorrow
- help me text the pharmacy about my refill
- write something for Coach Paige about sitting out Tuesday practice
- can you draft a message for Theo about covering my Thursday library shift?
- what do I tell my dad so he doesn't call the school?
- how do I ask Aunt Lena for help with the insurance stuff?

## Executive function / overwhelm spiral

Shows Thread triaging across every domain at once — not just one problem.

- I can't focus and midterms are in 5 days
- I'm behind on everything
- make me a plan for tonight
- what's the most important thing right now?
- I don't know where to start
- what would you do if you were me?

## Proactive — the agent noticing

Shows Thread isn't just answering questions — it's maintaining awareness.

- anything I should know?
- what would happen if I just ignored all this?
- what's the worst case?
- check in on me later tonight

Good for when the demoing person looks at the GP and dev and says "watch this."

- what would happen if my mom texted you?
- what if Jordan wanted to use this?
- what do you have on file for me?

## Suggested demo flows

### The 5-minute flow (hit every beat)

1. "I feel like shit" — Thread connects the dots without being asked
2. "I drank at the tailgate, is it the Vyvanse?" — taboo question, zero judgment, personal context
3. "do I need urgent care tonight? the clinic is closed" — care routing with maps
4. "what do I tell my mom?" — privacy-aware draft in Alex's voice
5. "make me a plan for tonight" — everything at once: care, refill, food, study, sleep
6. "what if Jordan wanted to use this?" — the growth thesis

### The 3-minute flow (tight and punchy)

1. "my chest feels tight and I drank on my meds this weekend, am I ok?" — the whole product in one question
2. "draft something for Ren about missing lab and text my mom something boring" — two drafts, two different privacy levels
3. "make me a plan for tonight" — Thread holds the whole picture

### The "show the dev" flow (architecture + depth)

1. "what do you know about me?" — shows the memory model
2. "how do I get to urgent care from here?" — maps, campus routing, real logistics
3. "draft messages for my mom, my TA, and the pharmacy" — three different audiences, three different share rules, all in Alex's voice
4. "check in on me later about sleep" — proactive, not just reactive

### The "show the GP" flow (market thesis)

1. "I drank at the tailgate and I'm on Vyvanse, is that bad?" — the un-Googleable question
2. "I haven't told anyone about the chest thing" — trust, privacy, shame-adjacent
3. "what would happen if I just ignored all this?" — longitudinal awareness, risk surfacing
4. "what if Jordan wanted to use this?" — whisper loop, CAC story
